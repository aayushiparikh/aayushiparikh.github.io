My Idea

The University From Which I Graduated Did Not Encourage Non-technical Events, Not Even During College-festival. So I Persuaded Them To Have Atleast One Non-technical Event, They Agreed To Try My Proposal For Intra-college Fest But With A Condition That It Should Be Unique, Something That None Of The University Had Tried Before And A Target Number Of Participants Was Given To Me. Therefore I Came Up With An Event Known As "Auction Dumb Charades". On The First Day There Were Very Few Registration. .

Later That Day I Came To Know That Students Were Not Clear About The Event. So Next Day, I Went To Each And Every Class And Explained Them In Detail What It Was About. As A Result There Was Huge Number Of Registrations. And Not Only Was The Target Audience Achieved But Almost Double The Number Of People Registered For It. The Event Was A Huge Success Which Lead The Admin To Add Non-technical Events In Intra College Festival

I Failed And Learnt

In College, We Started A Club Known As 'spark' Through Which We Wanted To Engage With School Students And Wanted To Conduct An Interschool Debate Or Group Discussion Competition. We Pitched The Idea To Sponsors And They Loved It And Decided To Fund Us. We Wanted To Start This Project At A Small Scale So We Got 5 Schools On Board. While The Project Was Still In The Planning Stage And Everything Was Communicated Verbally, Everyone Agreed. But When The Final Dates Was Set, 3 Schools Backed Out As One Had Their Own Annual Festival At That Time While The Other Two Had Exams During That Time. Now The Sponsor Was Not Willing To Do With Only 2 Schools. So We Had To Drop The Whole Project.

This Taught Me That We Should Not Have Had Limited Ourselves To 5 Schools, Instead, We Should Have Had Kept Some Of The Schools As Our Backup. So That We Could Have Continued The Event Even If Some Of The School Backouts At The Last Moment. This Taught Me To Always Have A Backup In Life.
